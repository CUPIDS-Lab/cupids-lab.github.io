CUPIDS partners with Colorado's media organizations, civic groups, legal aid clinics, tribal advocates, and professional associations to build lasting data capacity for public accountability. We collaborate three ways: **sponsored projects** scoped to your reporting or advocacy, **in-kind contributions** of data, access, or expertise, and **joint funding proposals** that pair your mission with our technical capacity.

Our [collaboration with the Center for Environmental Journalism](/projects/cej/) — backed by the [2026 de Castro Research Award](/dispatch/2026-06-16-de-castro-award/) — is the model. Browse our [open resources](/resources/), see [what the help desk does](/get-involved/#request-help), then [start a conversation](/get-involved/#inquire) or email [brian.keegan@colorado.edu](mailto:brian.keegan@colorado.edu).
