Philanthropic support builds capacity that outlasts any single grant. Funders can endow **named research positions** — the graduate, visiting, and undergraduate appointments described under [Join the lab](/get-involved/#join-the-lab) — or sponsor **data-archiving infrastructure** that keeps at-risk public datasets accessible to journalists and civic groups statewide.

Seed gifts also position the lab for competitive external funding: the [2026 de Castro Research Award](/dispatch/2026-06-16-de-castro-award/) launched our [Center for Environmental Journalism collaboration](/projects/cej/) on exactly that model. We work with **CU Advancement** to align philanthropic priorities with public-interest outcomes. Review our [research](/research/) and [resources](/resources/), then [reach out](/get-involved/#inquire) or email [brian.keegan@colorado.edu](mailto:brian.keegan@colorado.edu).
