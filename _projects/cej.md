The collaboration's first build is the **[Colorado Environmental Data Hub](https://github.com/CUPIDS-Lab/co-environmental-data)** — a documented, reproducible home for the public environmental data Colorado relies on, as federal and state sources are quietly removed or locked behind paywalls. The goal: make every source independently verifiable, and eventually map which reporters cite which datasets.

Working with CEJ director {% person hong-tien-vu %}, the lab's undergraduate researchers catalog each source, flag how at-risk it is, then "liberate" the priority ones into clean, auto-refreshing tables archived with a citable DOI.

Delivered so far: a catalog of 56 authoritative sources across six themes, and a working reservoir-storage pipeline tracking 130-plus Colorado reservoirs day by day. Streamflow and snowpack pipelines are next.

Follow the work in the repo's [changelog and roadmap](https://github.com/CUPIDS-Lab/co-environmental-data/blob/main/ROADMAP.md), or [request help](/get-involved/).
