A computational social scientist with eighteen years studying how people
collaborate online — on Wikipedia, Reddit, Twitter, and beyond. That research
has always depended on open data, and on watching access to it systematically
erode as platforms enclosed the information commons.

He has taught "Information Expositions" for eight years, serves as Vice Chair
of one of the nation's first cannabis licensing boards, and writes a
data-driven column for the Boulder Reporting Lab. CUPIDS is the synthesis of
that research, teaching, and civic work.
